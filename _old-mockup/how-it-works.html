How It Works

Four steps. One independent record.

Every inspection follows the same process, whether it's a single site or a hundred-location portfolio — because consistency is what makes a record defensible.

01 · Book

Tell us the site and the standard

Submit the address, occupancy type, and the code or standard you need verified — a single site or a full portfolio. We confirm scope and schedule within one business day.

02 · Inspect

An independent inspector visits on site

Arrival is geo-tagged automatically. Our inspector has no financial stake in what they find — they don't sell equipment, installs, or repair contracts.

03 · Document

Findings are timestamped as they happen

Every checkpoint — extinguisher charge, alarm test, exit path, sprinkler head — is logged on site with a photo, a timestamp, and a pass/fail mark. Nothing is reconstructed from memory later.

04 · File

Your report is filed the same day

The finished record goes to your client portal and, where required, the local authority having jurisdiction — before the inspector's day is over.

What "On Record" Means

Every visit leaves an evidentiary trail.

A compliance report is only as good as the trust behind it. That's why every Shield Fire Compliance inspection produces a record built to hold up — to your insurer, your board, or your local fire marshal.

  • • GPS location captured at time of arrival
  • • Date and time stamped on every finding
  • • Photo evidence attached to each checkpoint
  • • Pass/fail status recorded item by item
  • • Filed same-day — no backlog, no delay
